Synthetic fibers like Polyester and Nylon are hydrophobic (repel water), trapping sweat and bacteria against the skin. Formaldehyde resins and dyes are also common irritants.

Natural: Breathable, porous, and biodegradable (Cotton, Silk).
Synthetic: Durable but often traps heat and moisture (Acrylic, Polyester).

  • Wash new clothes before wearing.
  • Use hypoallergenic detergents.
  • Stick to White/Undyed organic cotton for eczema.

Frequently Asked Questions

100% Organic Cotton and Bamboo are widely considered the best options as they are breathable, soft, and lack harsh chemical treatments.

Wool fibers have microscopic scales that can prick the skin. If you love wool, try Merino wool, which has much finer fibers and is less irritating.

Often, wrinkle-free clothes are treated with formaldehyde-releasing resins, which can cause Contact Dermatitis in sensitive individuals.
